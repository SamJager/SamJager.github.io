Compact Heat Exchanger Design & Optimization

Thermal performance optimization for industrial cooling systems

Date: January 2026 Category: Thermal Analysis | CAD Design Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent, MATLAB
← Back to Projects

Project Overview

This project involved the design and optimization of a compact plate-fin heat exchanger for an industrial cooling application. The primary objective was to maximize heat transfer efficiency while minimizing pressure drop and overall size, all within strict manufacturing constraints.

Tools & Technologies Used

SolidWorks ANSYS Fluent MATLAB Python CFD Analysis

Problem Statement

The existing heat exchanger design was underperforming, leading to reduced system efficiency and increased energy costs. The challenge was to redesign the unit with the following requirements:

  • Increase heat transfer rate by minimum 25% without increasing footprint
  • Reduce pressure drop by at least 15% to lower pumping power requirements
  • Maintain compatibility with existing mounting and piping infrastructure
  • Use cost-effective materials suitable for the operating temperature range (0-150°C)
  • Ensure manufacturability with standard fabrication techniques

Design & Development Process

1. Research & Analysis

Initial analysis involved studying various heat exchanger configurations including plate-fin, shell-and-tube, and microchannel designs. Thermal-hydraulic calculations were performed using MATLAB to establish baseline performance metrics and identify the most promising configuration for the application.

2. Concept Development

Three distinct fin geometries were developed and evaluated: offset strip fins, louvered fins, and wavy fins. After preliminary CFD analysis, the offset strip fin design showed the best balance of heat transfer enhancement and acceptable pressure drop characteristics.

3. Detailed Design

The detailed design phase focused on optimizing key parameters:

  • CAD modeling in SolidWorks with parametric design for rapid iteration
  • Material selection: Aluminum 6061-T6 for optimal thermal conductivity and corrosion resistance
  • Fin pitch: 3.2 mm, fin height: 8.5 mm, fin thickness: 0.3 mm
  • Channel dimensions optimized through CFD sensitivity studies
  • Manufacturing tolerances specified per ASME standards

Analysis & Validation

Simulation & Analysis

Comprehensive CFD analysis was performed using ANSYS Fluent:

  • 3D conjugate heat transfer analysis with k-ε turbulence model
  • Operating conditions: Hot fluid 120°C, cold fluid 20°C, flow rates 2.5 L/s
  • Mesh refinement study conducted to ensure solution independence (3.2M cells)
  • Parametric studies on fin geometry yielded 32% improvement in heat transfer coefficient
  • Pressure drop reduced by 18% compared to baseline design

Testing & Validation

A prototype was fabricated and tested in a controlled laboratory environment:

  • Thermal performance tested across full operating range
  • Measured heat transfer rate within 5% of CFD predictions
  • Pressure drop measurements validated simulation results (7% deviation)
  • Long-term reliability testing completed with no degradation over 1000 hours

Results & Outcomes

The optimized heat exchanger design successfully met all project objectives:

  • Achieved 32% increase in heat transfer rate (exceeded 25% requirement)
  • Reduced pressure drop by 18% (exceeded 15% target)
  • Maintained same overall footprint as original design
  • Material costs increased only 8% while energy savings projected at $12,000/year
  • Successfully integrated with existing system infrastructure

Key Learnings

This project provided valuable insights into thermal system design:

  • Enhanced proficiency in CFD modeling and validation techniques
  • Developed systematic approach to multi-objective optimization problems
  • Gained experience in design-for-manufacturing considerations
  • Learned importance of experimental validation for complex thermal systems
  • Improved skills in technical documentation and results presentation

Future Improvements

Several opportunities for further optimization have been identified:

  • Investigation of advanced fin geometries using topology optimization
  • Evaluation of enhanced surface coatings for improved heat transfer
  • Development of adaptive flow control for variable load conditions
  • Integration of real-time monitoring sensors for predictive maintenance
